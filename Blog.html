Multilateration (MLAT)

To begin this journey I would like to introduce you to a fantastic localisation technique. It is used on airports around the world, and it was used by different kinds of navigation systems before the existence of GNSS.
The principle is quite simple: There is an object (let it be an aircraft) that creates a noise or a signal. This signal is received by different receivers spreaded on the ground and without knowing the initial time of transmission of the signal, we must estimate where the object is located.

To keep it even more simple, we will deal with a 2D prespective. We know that the first receiver listening the signal is the one closest to the aircraft/emitter, so we take that receiver as a reference. Then, we can receive the same signal emitted from the same aircraft from two other receivers. As they are further away, there will be a small delay on the reception of the signal.



Let's see how the intersection of the circles results on the estimation of the emitter position:

What's more important, it is that from the intersecting points of the circle of reference with the other circles creates a hyperbola. From intersection of hyperbolas, we get the estimated position of an emitter/aircraft!!!
